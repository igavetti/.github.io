About Me

I am currently pursuing a Research Master's in Brain and Cognitive Sciences at the University of Amsterdam. My academic background encompasses Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence. My academic interests focus on computational neuroscience and machine/deep learning, where I aim to contribute to advancing our understanding of brain function and artificial intelligence.

Why I Would Make a Strong PhD Candidate

My academic journey has taken me through renowned universities, where I’ve had the privilege to learn from leading researchers in their respective fields. I took Python programming classes from Prof. dr. ir. Pieter Spronck, a Computer Vision course taught by Prof. dr. Eric Postma, and both introductory and advanced classes in Neural Cognitive Modeling by Dr. Raquel Garrido Alhama and Dr. Willem Zuidema. Additionally, I deepened my understanding of neuroimaging through a BOLD fMRI class led by Dr. Steven Scholte and Dr. Joe Bathelt.

The focus throughout my studies has always been on applying what I have learned, giving me the opportunity to work on real-world problems and explore a broad spectrum of topics, from neuroscience to artificial intelligence. This academic experience has not only provided me with a solid theoretical foundation but also allowed me to develop practical skills, particularly in Python coding, machine learning, and computational modeling. These experiences have equipped me with the tools to approach complex problems analytically and find solutions effectively, which I believe are essential skills for any research role.

On a personal level, I always aim to be proactive and adaptable. I have a good sense of what is needed in different work situations, and I enjoy planning and organizing my work to ensure progress. I believe these qualities, combined with my academic background and technical expertise, make me well-prepared for the demands and challenges of a PhD program.
