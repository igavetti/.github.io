About Me

I am originally from northern Italy and am currently pursuing a Research Master’s in Brain and Cognitive Sciences at the University of Amsterdam. My background spans Cognitive Science and Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on computational neuroscience. My goal is to contribute to advancing our understanding of brain function and artificial intelligence.

My academic journey has provided me with a blend of interdisciplinary skills, research experience, and practical knowledge. Below are key factors which I belive would make a strong PhD candidate:

1. Research Projects

Throughout my master’s, I have been / will be involved in several research projects that honed my computational and analytical skills. For example, my first research project at Radboud University explored interpretable functional-genetic models of the zebrafish brain, working with data collected through light-sheet microscopy.

2. Literature Thesis

My literature review on structure-aware self-supervised learning for EEG data has been deepening my understanding of electroencephalography structure and self-supervised learning. I am working closely with Prof. Marijn van Wingerden, further strengthening my research and academic writing skills.

3. Interdisciplinarity

My current master's program has been encouraging me to take an interdisciplinary approach to research, allowing me to merge knowledge from (computational) neuroscience, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence. This interdisciplinary foundation is preparing me to tackle problems in neuroscience from multiple angles.

4. Extracurricular Activities

Outside of coursework, I am actively involved in various extracurricular activities, such as contributing to the Amsterdam Brain and Cognition Journal. Through this involvement, my goal is to strengthen my writing skills, learn about the peer-review prcoess and contribute towards open-access science.

Research Interests

I am passionate about the intersection of neuroscience and artificial intelligence. Below are some of the research areas I would be most excited to explore in a PhD journey:

  • Computational Models of Memory: Investigating how, e.g., time is encoded in memory traces and understanding the temporal dynamics of learning and recall.
  • Algorithms for High-Dimensional Data: Creating algorithms to extract insights from high-dimensional neural and genetic datasets.
  • Personalized Medicine: Developing predictive models to tailor treatments based on individual genetic, neural, and behavioral data.
  • Neuro-AI Interfaces: Designing AI systems that integrate with neural processes to achieve biologically plausible, interpretable outcomes.
